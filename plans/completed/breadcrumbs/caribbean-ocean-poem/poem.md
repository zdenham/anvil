# Caribbean Ocean

Turquoise water breaks on bone-white sand,
the sun pours gold across the shallows.
A pelican folds and drops like a dark hand
into the reef where the grouper wallows.

Salt wind carries steel drum and pepper smoke,
hibiscus trembles on the hill.
The sea mutters in the voice of older folk —
tide pulling out, tide pushing still.

At dusk the water turns to hammered bronze,
fishing boats rock on their chains.
Stars appear above the mangrove fronds
and the ocean hums its old refrains.
